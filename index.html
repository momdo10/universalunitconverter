Units are the standard way we measure and communicate quantities—such as meters for length, kilograms for weight, and seconds for time. These units help ensure that measurements are consistent and understood universally, no matter where you are.

Unit systems, like the Metric System or the Imperial System, organize groups of these units and define how they relate to one another. They are essential in science, engineering, trade, and daily life to avoid confusion and ensure accuracy.
